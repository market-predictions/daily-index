The technical overlay is only mixed rather than strongly supportive, but it is not bearish. USD remains the highest-quality base sleeve.

**JPY — Hold / stage**  
The BoJ still guides the uncollateralized overnight call rate around 0.75%, and the macro haven thesis remains intact. But the overlay still shows USDJPY technically positive, which means JPY is not currently confirming the strategic case. JPY remains retained-but-trimmed rather than rebuilt.

**CHF — Build on weakness**  
The SNB policy rate is 0.00%. CHF is not a carry currency, but it remains one of the cleanest defensive diversifiers. With USDCHF only mixed rather than strongly adverse, CHF stays ahead of JPY as the second-line defensive sleeve.

**GBP — Reduce**  
Current Bank Rate is still 3.75%, but the BoE’s own February communication described subdued growth and a loosening labour market. GBPUSD technicals remain negative, so the macro and technical picture still justifies a reduction.

**AUD — Hold / stage**  
The RBA cash rate is 3.85% and the February policy package was materially hawkish. AUD remains cyclical and not a leadership currency in a mild risk-off world, but the overlay remains positive. That still justifies a staged allocation.

**CAD — Hold**  
The BoC policy rate is 2.25%. CAD’s main support is oil and terms of trade rather than policy leadership. Technicals are still mixed, which is consistent with a hold stance.

**NZD — Sell / avoid**  
The RBNZ held the OCR at 2.25% and said inflation should fall while the labor market remained weak or elevated in slack terms. The overlay is only mixed, which is not enough to challenge the weak strategic case.

**MXN — Hold**  
Banxico’s target rate remains 7.00%. The carry is still useful, but the overlay is only mixed, so the correct stance remains selective holding rather than aggressive adding.

**ZAR — Sell / avoid**  
The SARB repo rate is 6.75%, but ZAR remains the wrong kind of exposure for a defensive portfolio with geopolitical and trade-policy awareness. The overlay is only mixed and does not rescue the weak strategic case.
