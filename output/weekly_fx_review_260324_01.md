## 10. Current currency review

**USD — Buy**  
The Fed kept the fed funds target range at 3.50–3.75% on March 18, said activity was still expanding at a solid pace, and noted that uncertainty about the outlook remained elevated. The technical overlay is only mixed rather than strongly supportive, but it is not bearish. USD remains the highest-quality base sleeve.

**EUR — Reduce**  
The ECB deposit rate remains at 2.00%. Lower relative carry and renewed energy sensitivity keep EUR below the leadership tier. The technical overlay is negative on EURUSD, so macro and technical signals now align to the downside.

**JPY — Hold / stage**  
The BoJ still guides the uncollateralized overnight call rate around 0.75%, and the macro haven thesis remains intact. But the overlay shows USDJPY still technically positive, which means JPY is not currently confirming the strategic case. That moves JPY from high-conviction buy to retained-but-trimmed hold.

**CHF — Build on weakness**  
The SNB policy rate is 0.00%. CHF is not a carry currency, but it remains one of the cleanest defensive diversifiers. With USDCHF only mixed rather than strongly adverse, CHF now ranks ahead of JPY as the second-line defensive sleeve on this run.

**GBP — Reduce**  
Current Bank Rate is still 3.75%, but the BoE’s own February communication described subdued growth and a loosening labour market. GBPUSD technicals are negative, so the macro and technical picture together justify a reduction.

**AUD — Hold / stage**  
The RBA raised the cash rate to 3.85% in February and said inflation had picked up materially, with growth and labor-market conditions firmer than expected. AUD remains cyclical and not a leadership currency in a mild risk-off world, but the overlay is now positive. That earns AUD a staged increase rather than continued pure underweight treatment.

**CAD — Hold**  
The BoC policy rate is 2.25%. CAD’s main support is oil and terms of trade rather than policy leadership. Technicals are mixed, which is consistent with a hold stance.

**NZD — Sell / avoid**  
The RBNZ held the OCR at 2.25% and said inflation should fall while the labor market remained weak or elevated in slack terms. The overlay is only mixed, which is not enough to challenge the weak strategic case.

**MXN — Hold**  
Banxico’s target rate remains 7.00%. The carry is still useful, but the overlay is only mixed, so the correct stance remains selective holding rather than aggressive adding.

**ZAR — Sell / avoid**  
The SARB repo rate is 6.75%, but ZAR remains the wrong kind of exposure for a defensive portfolio with geopolitical and trade-policy awareness. The overlay is only mixed and does not rescue the weak strategic case.
